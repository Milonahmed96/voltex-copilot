PART 1 — WHAT IS VOLTCARE AND WHICH PLAN IS RIGHT FOR YOU
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

VoltCare is Voltex's own protection plan. It extends the cover you get from the manufacturer's
standard warranty and, depending on which tier you choose, adds protection against accidental
damage too. You can buy VoltCare at the time you purchase your product in store or online, or
within 30 days of your purchase date.

THREE TIERS, ONE FOR EVERY NEED

VoltCare Essential — peace of mind for everyday breakdowns
Covers mechanical and electrical faults that occur through normal use. If your product stops
working because something inside it has failed, we'll repair or replace it.
What it does NOT cover: accidental damage (drops, spills, cracks).
Available on: all product categories sold by Voltex.
Duration: 1 or 2 years (your choice at purchase).
Price: from £3.99 per month, or save 10% by paying upfront annually.

VoltCare Plus — for the moments life happens
Everything in Essential, plus cover for accidental damage — one accidental damage claim per
12-month rolling period. So if you drop your laptop or spill coffee on your phone, you're covered.
An excess applies to accidental damage claims (see Part 3 for amounts).
What it does NOT cover: white goods (washing machines, fridges, ovens — see note below).
Available on: laptops, phones, tablets, TVs, audio equipment, gaming consoles.
Duration: 2 or 3 years.
Price: from £6.99 per month.

NOTE FOR WHITE GOODS CUSTOMERS: VoltCare Plus is not available for washing machines,
fridge-freezers, dishwashers, or ovens. For these products, your options are Essential or
Complete. This is because accidental damage cover is not designed for fixed home appliances.

VoltCare Complete — our highest level of protection
Everything in Plus, with no limit on accidental damage claims and no excess to pay. We also
include an annual health check for eligible appliances. And if we can't fix your product within
48 hours of receiving it, we'll replace it — no arguments.
Available on: laptops priced £800 or over, TVs priced £600 or over, and selected white goods
(washing machines, fridge-freezers, and range cookers) priced £400 or over.
Duration: 3 years (fixed).
Price: from £14.99 per month.

IMPORTANT NOTE FOR COMPLETE WHITE GOODS CUSTOMERS: On white goods, Complete covers unlimited
breakdown repairs — not accidental damage (white goods are fixed appliances where accidental
damage is not a standard risk). The "unlimited claims" benefit means you can have multiple
breakdown faults repaired at no extra cost over your 3-year plan.

PART 3 — WHAT IS COVERED AND WHAT IS NOT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHAT ALL PLANS COVER
- Mechanical and electrical breakdown: any fault that occurs through normal use
- Faults caused by power surges
- Parts and labour for all covered repairs — you pay nothing beyond any applicable excess
- Software faults on computing devices: diagnostics and operating system reinstallation
  (note: Complete also covers this; Essential and Plus cover it where the fault is caused by
  a hardware issue rather than user-installed software)

WHAT PLUS AND COMPLETE ALSO COVER
- Accidental damage: damage caused by a single, identifiable accident — a drop, a spill,
  a crack from impact
- Plus: one accidental damage claim per rolling 12-month period
- Complete: unlimited accidental damage claims, no excess

EXCESS AMOUNTS FOR ACCIDENTAL DAMAGE (PLUS TIER ONLY — no excess on Complete)
Products purchased for under £300: £25 excess per accidental damage claim
Products purchased for £300–£799: £49 excess per accidental damage claim
Products purchased for £800 or over: £75 excess per accidental damage claim
The excess is collected before your repair is booked. You can pay by card over the phone,
online at voltex.co.uk/my-plans, or in any Voltex store.

CUSTOMERS WITH PLANS PURCHASED BEFORE 1 OCTOBER 2024
If you bought your VoltCare plan before 1 October 2024, your plan operates under our previous
terms (Version 4.5). Under Version 4.5, no excess applied to accidental damage claims on the
Plus tier. If you are asked to pay an excess and you believe your plan predates October 2024,
please ask us to check your plan version — we will verify this and correct any error immediately.
Your plan version is shown in your confirmation email and in your online account.

WHAT IS NOT COVERED UNDER ANY PLAN

Cosmetic damage: scratches, dents, scuffs, or discolouration that do not affect how your
product works are not covered. We cover faults, not wear to appearance.

Consumable parts: some components are designed to wear out over time and are the owner's
responsibility to replace. These include printer ink and toner cartridges, vacuum filters,
washing machine door seals (after normal use), oven heating elements (unless they fail
within the first 12 months), and laptop batteries that have degraded naturally after heavy use.

Data loss: VoltCare does not cover the loss of any data stored on your device. We strongly
recommend regular backups. Before sending any device for repair, please back up your data —
Voltex cannot be held responsible for data loss during the repair process.

Damage caused by pests: damage from mice, insects, or other pests is a matter for your home
insurance, not VoltCare.

Business use: VoltCare is a residential plan. If your product is used primarily for business
or commercial purposes, it is not eligible for VoltCare cover. If you are unsure whether your
use qualifies, please call us before making a claim.

Theft and loss: VoltCare does not cover theft or loss of any product. If you need cover for
theft, this is available through home contents insurance or, for phones, through VoltMobile
device insurance.

Intentional damage: damage caused deliberately by you or anyone else is not covered.

SPECIFIC SITUATIONS CUSTOMERS OFTEN ASK ABOUT

"My TV remote stopped working — is that covered?"
Remote controls are accessories and are not part of the TV unit covered by VoltCare. We sell
replacement and universal remotes in store and online from £9.99.

"My laptop fan is making a loud noise"
A noisy fan can be a mechanical fault (covered) or normal behaviour when your laptop is
working hard under load (not a fault). Our repair team will assess this. If it is confirmed
as a mechanical failure, it is covered under all tiers.

"My washing machine is shaking a lot"
Excessive vibration can be caused by the machine not being level, or by a bearing or drum
fault. The first is an installation issue and not covered; the second is a mechanical fault
and is covered. We will send an engineer to assess — we do not make this judgement over the phone.

"There are lines across my phone screen but I didn't drop it"
Screen faults can occur without any impact — this is a manufacturing or hardware fault, not
accidental damage. This is covered as a breakdown claim under all tiers, with no excess.
If you did drop the phone and the screen is now damaged, that is an accidental damage claim
(covered under Plus and Complete, not Essential).

"I spilled liquid on my laptop and some keys have stopped working"
Liquid damage is accidental damage. It is covered under Plus and Complete (subject to excess
on Plus). It is not covered under Essential. Please do not turn the device on after liquid
exposure — bring it to a Voltex store or request a collection as soon as possible.

"My OLED TV has an image burnt into the screen"
OLED burn-in is not covered under any VoltCare plan. This is a known characteristic of OLED
display technology, not a product fault, and is excluded by Voltex and by all manufacturers.
We recommend using your TV's built-in pixel refresh feature regularly to minimise this risk.

PART 4 — HOW TO MAKE A CLAIM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FOUR WAYS TO CLAIM

Online (fastest): voltex.co.uk/voltcare-claim — available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
By phone: 0333 400 7000, select option 2. Monday to Saturday 8am–8pm, Sunday 9am–5pm.
In store: visit any Voltex store and a colleague will initiate your claim for you.
Voltex app: open the app, go to My Plans, and select Claim (available for accounts
created after March 2023).

WHAT YOU WILL NEED
Your VoltCare plan reference number — this is in your plan confirmation email and your
online account. It starts with VC- followed by 8 digits.
Proof of purchase — your receipt, order confirmation email, or your Voltex account order history.
Your product's serial number — usually on the back or base of the product, or in the device
settings under "About". For washing machines it is typically inside the door rim.
A description of the fault or incident — what happened, when it happened, and what the
product is now doing (or not doing).

For accidental damage claims, we will also ask:
When did the incident happen?
What happened — for example, did the product fall, get wet, or get caught in a door?
Was this a single incident or repeated exposure over time? (A single event is covered;
gradual exposure such as moisture from a steamy bathroom over several months is not.)
For liquid damage to a phone: was the phone fully submerged, or was it a splash or spill?
Both are covered under Plus and Complete, but we ask to understand the extent of the damage.

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER YOU CLAIM
We will confirm your claim by SMS and email, including your claim reference number (starts CL-).
If an excess applies, we will collect this before booking your repair.
For small tech: we will book a courier to collect your product, or you can drop it at any store.
For white goods: we will book an engineer visit to your home.
You will receive repair progress updates by SMS.

IF WE NEED MORE INFORMATION
Occasionally we may need to ask you additional questions before we can approve your claim.
This is most common for accidental damage claims or where the product's repair history
is complex. We aim to resolve any queries within 2 business days. During this time your
claim is paused — the clock on our repair timescales does not start until the claim is approved.

SMALL TECH (PHONES, TABLETS, LAPTOPS, AUDIO, GAMING CONSOLES, TVs)

How we repair your product:
Collection from your home: we will book a courier to collect your product within 2 business
days of your claim being approved. Your product is sent to our Repair Centre in Newark,
Nottinghamshire — one of the largest tech repair facilities in Europe.
Drop off at a Voltex store: bring your product to any store before 2pm and it is dispatched
to the Repair Centre the same day.
In-store repair while you wait: available for selected phone screen replacements at our
47 Tech Bar stores (check voltex.co.uk/tech-bar for your nearest location). Most in-store
screen repairs take 60 to 90 minutes.

How long repairs take:
VoltCare Essential and Plus: we aim to complete your repair within 5 working days of
receiving your product at the Repair Centre.
VoltCare Complete: we guarantee to repair or replace your product within 48 business hours
of receiving it. If we miss this, you are entitled to a replacement or a full refund of
the current market value — whichever you prefer.

WHITE GOODS (WASHING MACHINES, FRIDGE-FREEZERS, DISHWASHERS, OVENS, RANGE COOKERS)

We repair white goods at your home — we do not collect large appliances.
After your claim is approved, we will book an engineer visit. Our target is to visit within
3 to 7 business days. On the first visit, the engineer will diagnose the fault. If the right
part is on the van, the repair will be completed the same day. If a part needs to be ordered,
a second visit will be booked. We aim to source parts within 3 to 5 business days.

If we cannot complete your white goods repair within 15 business days of your claim being
approved, you are entitled to choose one of the following:
A like-for-like replacement appliance, delivered and installed by Voltex at no charge.
A Voltex gift card to the value of the current market price of an equivalent appliance.
We will always present both options clearly — the choice is entirely yours.

IF YOUR FRIDGE OR FREEZER BREAKS DOWN (Complete customers)
If your fridge-freezer develops a fault that means you have no refrigeration while waiting
for repair, please call us. VoltCare Complete includes a contribution of up to £50 toward
temporary refrigeration or food spoilage costs. Please speak to our team to arrange this.

WHAT HAPPENS IF YOUR PRODUCT CANNOT BE REPAIRED
If our engineers assess that your product is beyond economical repair, we will contact you
and offer you the same two options as above: a like-for-like replacement, or a gift card
at current market value. We will explain our assessment and you can ask questions about it.

DELAYS OUTSIDE OUR CONTROL
Our timescales may be affected by circumstances we cannot control, such as a manufacturer
placing parts on global allocation due to supply chain disruption. In these cases we will
contact you, explain the delay, and give you an updated expected completion date. Your
rights under your plan remain the same — if we exceed the maximum timescale, your
replacement entitlement still applies.

MONTHLY PAYMENTS
Your plan renews each month by Direct Debit or continuous card payment.
If a payment fails, here is what happens:
Day 1: payment fails — we try again automatically in 3 days.
Day 4: if the second attempt fails, we notify you by email and SMS.
Day 10: we make a third and final automatic attempt.
Day 11 to Day 25 (Grace Period): if payment is still outstanding, your plan enters a
14-day Grace Period. Your cover continues in full during this time.
To reinstate your plan: update your payment details at voltex.co.uk/my-plans or call us.
Day 25 onwards: if payment remains outstanding after the Grace Period, your plan is
suspended and cover stops. You can still reinstate by paying the outstanding balance.
Day 55: if the balance is not paid by day 55, your plan is cancelled. If you wish to
protect your product after this point, you will need to purchase a new plan. Products
over 3 years old at the time of cancellation may not be eligible for a new VoltCare plan.

IMPORTANT: if you need to make a claim while your account is in the Grace Period (days 11–25),
your claim will proceed as normal. A payment problem does not suspend your right to claim
during the Grace Period.

ANNUAL PAYMENT (UPFRONT)
If you chose to pay annually upfront, you received a 10% discount. Your plan does not have
a monthly payment to fail. Cancellation terms are the same as for monthly plans (see below).

AUTO-RENEWAL
Your plan renews automatically when it reaches the end of its term, unless you cancel.
We will email you (and write to you for annual plans) 45 days before your renewal date,
confirming the renewal price. Prices may change at renewal.
If you do not want to renew, cancel before your renewal date via My Plans online, by phone,
or in store.
If your plan has already renewed and you change your mind within 14 days of the renewal
date, contact us — we will refund the renewed payment in full.

CANCELLING YOUR PLAN

Within 45 days of purchase (cooling-off period):
If you have not made any claims, you receive a full refund of everything you have paid.
If you have made a claim, your refund is calculated as: total amount paid, minus the value
of any repair or replacement we provided. If we spent more on your claim than you paid in,
no refund is due — but you will not be charged anything further.

After 45 days:
You can cancel at any time. Your refund is calculated as: the value of your remaining plan
months (pro-rata), minus a £15 administration fee, minus the value of any claims we have paid.
If total claim value exceeds total plan value, no refund is due and no further charges apply.

Example: You paid £120 for a 2-year plan and used 8 months (£40 equivalent). We repaired
your laptop at a cost of £180 to Voltex. No refund is due, as the claim value exceeds
the plan value. You keep the repair — you simply do not receive a refund on cancellation.

How to cancel: call 0333 400 7000, visit any Voltex store, or write to:
VoltCare Customer Relations, Voltex Retail Ltd, 12 Salford Innovation Park, Salford, M50 2UU.

IF YOUR PRODUCT HAS BEEN MODIFIED OR REPAIRED ELSEWHERE
VoltCare cover is voided if your product has been opened and repaired or modified by anyone
other than Voltex, a Voltex-authorised repairer, or the original manufacturer. Signs of this
include broken tamper-evident seals, mismatched fixings, or third-party components.
If your gaming console or mobile device has had unofficial firmware installed (jailbreaking,
rooting, or custom software), your VoltCare cover is voided for faults arising from or
connected to that modification.
If Voltex's repair team identifies signs of previous unauthorised work, they will contact you
before proceeding and explain your options.

IF YOU ARE GIVEN THE PRODUCT AS A GIFT OR BUY IT SECOND-HAND
VoltCare purchased at the point of sale can be transferred to a new owner if the product
changes hands. To transfer a plan:
Register the transfer at voltex.co.uk/voltcare-transfer within 30 days of the ownership change.
A £15 transfer fee applies. This fee is waived if the product was given as a gift and you
can provide a gift receipt or equivalent documentation.
If you and the previous owner live at the same address and are immediate family members,
no transfer registration is required — the plan automatically covers the household.
If a transfer has not been registered and a claim is made by the new owner, the claim
will not be valid. We recommend all buyers of second-hand Voltex products check whether
an active VoltCare plan exists and register the transfer promptly.

YOUR STATUTORY RIGHTS
VoltCare is a paid protection plan, but it does not replace your statutory rights under
UK law. Under the Consumer Rights Act 2015:
Within 30 days of purchase: if your product is faulty, you have the right to a full refund.
This is a return, not a VoltCare claim, and is processed separately.
Within 6 months of purchase: if a fault appears, Voltex must repair or replace the product.
If a repair or replacement is not possible or fails, you may be entitled to a price reduction
or full refund. VoltCare does not remove or reduce these rights.
After 6 months: you still have rights, but you may need to show the fault was present from
the start. VoltCare can make this easier, as we cover faults without requiring you to
prove origin.
If you are unsure whether to use VoltCare or your statutory rights for a particular situation,
please ask us — we will always explain both options honestly.
