PART 1 — WHAT IS VOLTMOBILE AND HOW IT WORKS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

VoltMobile is Voltex's own mobile network service. We provide mobile calls, texts,
and data to customers across the UK. VoltMobile operates as a Mobile Virtual Network
Operator (MVNO), which means we use EE's physical network infrastructure — the masts,
cables, and spectrum — while operating our own billing, customer service, and plans.

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR YOU AS A CUSTOMER

Coverage: VoltMobile uses EE's network, which has the widest 4G coverage in the UK
and extensive 5G coverage in major towns and cities. You can check coverage at
voltex.co.uk/voltmobile-coverage or by entering your postcode in the VoltMobile app.

Network issues and outages: if EE's network experiences a technical fault or planned
maintenance, VoltMobile customers are affected. VoltMobile customer services cannot
resolve network infrastructure issues directly — we report them to EE and provide
updates. Planned maintenance is published at voltex.co.uk/network-status.

What VoltMobile controls: your plan, billing, account management, customer service,
add-ons, upgrades, and device purchases. Contact VoltMobile for all of these.

What VoltMobile does not control: signal strength in specific locations (determined
by EE's network infrastructure), international roaming agreements with overseas
operators, and national network outages.

AGENT NOTE: if a customer calls about poor signal or no service in a specific location,
check the network status page before assuming it is a handset or SIM issue. If the
network status page shows no issues, the next step is to check whether the issue is
location-specific (a known coverage gap) or device-specific (try SIM in another device).
Do not promise a signal fix — coverage gaps can only be resolved by EE's network
investment programme, not by VoltMobile.

VOLTMOBILE PLAN TYPES

SIM-Only Plans
A SIM card only — no handset included. Customer uses their existing phone.
Available on:
  - Monthly rolling (30-day notice to cancel): no minimum term, full flexibility.
  - 12-month contract: lower monthly price in exchange for a minimum term commitment.
  - 24-month contract: lowest monthly price; early termination charges apply.
Data allowances: 5GB, 15GB, 30GB, 60GB, 100GB, and Unlimited.
All SIM-only plans include unlimited UK calls and unlimited UK texts as standard.
5G access: included at no extra charge on plans 30GB and above, subject to 5G coverage.
On plans under 30GB, 5G is available as a £3/month add-on.

Phone Plans (handset + SIM)
A new smartphone bundled with a VoltMobile SIM plan. The handset cost is spread across
the contract term — customers pay a monthly amount covering both the service and the
device. This is a regulated credit arrangement under the Consumer Credit Act 1974.
Available terms: 24 months (standard), 36 months (lower monthly cost, longer commitment).
Handset brands available: Apple iPhone, Samsung Galaxy, Google Pixel, Nothing Phone.
An upfront cost (deposit) may apply depending on the handset value and the customer's
credit assessment. The upfront cost is shown clearly at checkout and cannot be waived.
Credit assessment: all phone plan applications are subject to a credit check by our
lending partner, Duologi Finance Ltd. Customers who do not pass the credit check are
offered a SIM-only plan instead. Credit decisions cannot be overridden by Voltex agents.

Prepay (Pay As You Go)
No monthly contract. Customer tops up credit and pays per use.
Available in store only. Not available online or by phone.
Bundles: prepay customers can purchase monthly bundles (calls, texts, and data for
a fixed price) that expire after 30 days. Unused bundle allowance does not roll over.
Prepay plans do not include roaming — international calls and texts are charged at
prepay rates, which are significantly higher than contract rates.

DATA ALLOWANCES AND FAIR USE

Standard data allowances are for UK use. Using your allowance in the UK works as stated
on your plan — there are no speed caps or throttling during normal use.

Unlimited data plans: VoltMobile's Unlimited plans provide genuinely unlimited data
for personal use. A fair use policy applies to prevent use that adversely affects
the network for other customers:
  - Tethering (using your phone as a Wi-Fi hotspot) is permitted up to 30GB per month
    on Unlimited plans. Above 30GB of tethering, speeds may be reduced.
  - Using a SIM in a router or other non-phone device is not permitted on SIM-only plans.
    A dedicated data SIM is required for this use — ask about our VoltMobile Data SIM plans.
  - Commercial use of consumer SIM plans (reselling data, running a business service
    from a consumer SIM) is not permitted and will result in plan suspension.

Data rollover: unused monthly data does not roll over to the next month on any plan
except the VoltMobile Flex plans (30GB and 60GB Flex only — see below).

VoltMobile Flex Plans: available on 30GB and 60GB monthly rolling plans. Unused data
from one month rolls over for a maximum of one additional month. Rolled-over data is
used before current month's allowance. Rolled-over data cannot be gifted or transferred.

DATA ADD-ONS
If a customer runs out of data before the end of their billing month:
  - A data boost of 1GB (£2.50), 3GB (£5.00), or 5GB (£7.50) can be purchased
    in the VoltMobile app or by calling 0333 400 9000.
  - Data boosts expire at the end of the current billing month — unused boost data
    is not carried over.
  - Data boosts are available on all contract plans. Not available on prepay
    (prepay customers must purchase a new bundle).

WITHOUT A DATA ADD-ON: once a customer's data allowance is exhausted, mobile data
is paused — the customer is not automatically charged for additional data. They
receive an SMS notification when at 80% and again at 100% of their allowance.
AGENT NOTE: some customers call believing they have been charged for excess data.
Under VoltMobile's plan structure, this cannot happen on UK data — data is capped,
not charged-over. If the customer sees an unexpected charge, it is more likely to be
a roaming charge, a premium rate call, or a third-party charge (see Part 7).

INTERNATIONAL CALLS AND TEXTS FROM THE UK (TO OVERSEAS NUMBERS)

Calling or texting an overseas number while in the UK (e.g. calling a family member
abroad from a UK location) is not included in standard plan allowances.
Rates for international calls from UK:
  - Republic of Ireland: 5p per minute (all plans)
  - EU countries: 12p per minute (all plans)
  - USA and Canada: 8p per minute (all plans)
  - Rest of world: 25p–£1.50 per minute depending on destination
International texts from UK: 25p per text to any international number.
International calling add-on: £5/month — includes 100 minutes to EU, USA, Canada,
and Republic of Ireland. Available on all contract plans.

This section covers using your VoltMobile SIM in another country.
Roaming is a significant source of customer confusion and unexpected charges.
Agents must understand the difference between EU roaming, rest-of-world roaming,
and the plan tier roaming inclusions before advising customers.

UK NETWORKS AND EU ROAMING — POST-BREXIT CONTEXT

Since the UK left the EU, UK mobile networks are no longer required to offer
free EU roaming as they were under EU regulations. VoltMobile's roaming policy
depends on the plan tier:

Plans 60GB and above (including all Unlimited plans):
EU roaming is included at no extra charge. You can use your UK data, calls, and
texts allowance in any EU country as if you were in the UK.
Fair use limit applies: up to 15GB of EU roaming data per month on plans under
Unlimited. On Unlimited plans, the same 30GB tethering cap applies abroad.
Covered EU destinations: all 27 EU member states plus Iceland, Liechtenstein,
Norway, and Switzerland (the EEA countries).
NOTE: EU roaming inclusion does NOT extend to Turkey, Morocco, Egypt, or other
non-EEA countries that are popular holiday destinations. Customers frequently
assume these are included — always check the destination specifically.

Plans under 60GB (5GB, 15GB, 30GB SIM-only and equivalent phone plans):
EU roaming is NOT included as standard. A Roaming Pass is required.
EU Roaming Pass: £10 for 14 days. Includes 10GB data, unlimited calls and texts
within the EU. Can be purchased in the VoltMobile app or by calling 0333 400 9000.
Must be purchased before travel — it cannot be applied retrospectively to charges
already incurred.

WITHOUT A ROAMING PASS ON PLANS UNDER 60GB:
Data: £0.50 per MB (equates to £500 per GB — extremely expensive).
Calls received: £0.20 per minute.
Calls made within the visited country: £0.60 per minute.
Calls back to the UK: £1.20 per minute.
Texts sent: £0.30 per text.
A roaming data cap of £30 applies — once £30 of roaming charges is reached,
data roaming is automatically suspended. The cap does not apply to calls and texts.
AGENT NOTE: if a customer has incurred significant roaming charges, check whether
they were on a plan that should have included roaming but did not due to a plan
configuration error. If a Voltex agent mis-sold the plan or failed to inform the
customer of roaming costs, a goodwill adjustment may be appropriate — escalate to
Team Leader with full account history.

REST OF WORLD ROAMING

Countries outside the EU/EEA are divided into three roaming zones:
Zone A (USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, UAE, Israel):
  Roaming Pass A: £12 for 14 days. Includes 5GB data, unlimited calls and texts
  to the local network and back to the UK.

Zone B (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, South Africa, Brazil, Mexico, Malaysia,
Singapore, Thailand, Philippines, Indonesia, Kenya, Nigeria, Jamaica):
  Roaming Pass B: £15 for 14 days. Includes 3GB data, unlimited calls and texts.

Zone C (all other destinations not listed in Zone A or B):
  Roaming Pass C: £20 for 14 days. Includes 2GB data; calls and texts at
  the rates listed on voltex.co.uk/voltmobile-roaming.
  Some destinations in Zone C have restricted data availability due to local
  network limitations — check the destination page before travel.

Countries where VoltMobile roaming is NOT available:
Cuba, North Korea, Iran, Syria, and a small number of other destinations due to
sanctions or lack of network agreements. Customers travelling to these destinations
cannot use their VoltMobile SIM and should purchase a local SIM on arrival.
Current full list: voltex.co.uk/voltmobile-roaming-restricted.

ROAMING TIPS FOR AGENTS ADVISING CUSTOMERS BEFORE TRAVEL
Always confirm the specific country or countries the customer will visit.
Check whether the destination is EU/EEA, Zone A, B, or C, or restricted.
Confirm the customer's current plan — determine whether EU roaming is included.
If a Roaming Pass is needed, advise the customer to purchase before departure.
Remind the customer that Roaming Passes cannot be applied retrospectively.
For customers visiting multiple countries across different zones: they need a
separate Roaming Pass for each zone, or the pass covering their primary destination.

PART 5 — NUMBER PORTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Number porting allows you to bring your existing mobile number to VoltMobile from
your current network. This is a regulated process governed by Ofcom rules.

HOW TO PORT YOUR NUMBER TO VOLTMOBILE

Step 1 — Obtain a PAC code from your current network.
A PAC (Porting Authorisation Code) is a unique code your current network must provide
within 2 hours of your request, at any time 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. This is an
Ofcom requirement — your current network cannot refuse or delay providing a PAC.
You can request a PAC by texting PAC to 65075 (free, works on any UK network)
or by calling your current network directly.
Your PAC is valid for 30 days from the date of issue.

Step 2 — Provide the PAC to VoltMobile when activating your SIM.
Online: enter during checkout at voltex.co.uk/voltmobile.
In store: give the PAC to the colleague activating your SIM.
By phone: 0333 400 9000.

Step 3 — Porting timescale.
Porting typically completes by midnight on the first working day after VoltMobile
submits the port request to EE and your current network. You will receive an SMS
from VoltMobile confirming the port completion time.
During the porting window: there may be a period (typically 2 to 4 hours) during
which your number is temporarily unavailable on both networks while the port completes.
This is normal. If your number is unavailable for more than 24 hours, contact us.

Step 4 — After porting is complete.
Your old SIM from your previous network will stop working. Insert your VoltMobile
SIM if you have not already done so. Restart your phone after inserting the SIM.

PORTING AWAY FROM VOLTMOBILE

If you wish to take your number to a different network:
Text PAC to 65075 (free) or call 0333 400 9000 to request your PAC.
VoltMobile will provide your PAC within 2 hours as required by Ofcom.
AGENT NOTE: we are required to provide the PAC promptly. We should not attempt to
retain the customer by delaying the PAC or making the process unnecessarily difficult.
We may offer the customer a retention deal — a better plan or price — but only after
confirming we will provide the PAC and that the decision is theirs. We must not make
the retention offer conditional on the customer not receiving their PAC.

PORTING ERRORS
If a customer's number has not ported correctly (wrong number ported, porting failed,
or number is in a perpetual unavailable state):
This is a priority case. Log immediately in CRM and escalate to the Porting Team.
The Porting Team has direct contact with EE's porting systems and can resolve most
porting errors within 4 business hours.
Do not ask the customer to wait standard response times for a porting error — their
number being unavailable is a significant service failure.

UPGRADING YOUR PLAN OR HANDSET

SIM-only plan upgrade (moving to a higher data tier or different plan):
Available at any time. Upgrades take effect from the next billing date.
Downgrading: available on monthly rolling plans at any time.
On 12-month or 24-month contracts: downgrading to a lower data tier is not permitted
mid-contract. The customer must wait until the contract end date.

Phone plan upgrade (new handset before contract end):
Early upgrade is available from month 18 of a 24-month contract, subject to:
  - Account in good standing (no missed payments in the last 3 months)
  - Credit reassessment (a new credit check is conducted for the new handset agreement)
Early upgrade before month 18: not available as standard. Team Leader can authorise
an exception in very limited circumstances — for example, a handset that is genuinely
beyond repair and the customer has no VoltCare claim available.
Early upgrade process: the remaining balance on the current handset agreement is
settled (either paid by the customer or rolled into the new agreement — customer choice).
The new agreement starts immediately.

END OF CONTRACT NOTIFICATION
VoltMobile sends a notification 45 days before contract end by SMS and email,
explaining the customer's options: upgrade, switch to a SIM-only plan, or cancel.
At the contract end date, phone plan customers who take no action are automatically
moved to a monthly rolling SIM-only equivalent plan at the prevailing rate.
The handset is fully owned by the customer at contract end — no further payments
are due on the device.

CANCELLATION — SIM-ONLY MONTHLY ROLLING PLANS

30 days notice required. Notice can be given:
By texting CANCEL to 88020 (free)
By calling 0333 400 9000
Via the VoltMobile app under Account > Cancel plan
The plan continues through the 30-day notice period and the customer is billed normally
for this final month. The SIM is deactivated at the end of the notice period.
Refunds are not given for the notice period — the customer has service throughout.

CANCELLATION — SIM-ONLY 12-MONTH AND 24-MONTH CONTRACTS

Within 14 days of the contract start date (cooling-off period):
Full cancellation with no early termination charge. Return the SIM (or destroy it —
no physical return required). Any usage charges incurred during the 14-day period
(calls, texts, data used) are billed and must be paid.
After 14 days:
Early termination charge applies. The charge is calculated as:
  Sum of remaining monthly payments × 0.97 (a 3% discount applied per Ofcom guidance).
  Example: 10 months remaining at £15/month = £150 × 0.97 = £145.50 early termination charge.
The early termination charge covers the service portion of the contract only.

CANCELLATION — PHONE PLANS (HANDSET + SIM)

Phone plans consist of two elements: the service agreement and the handset credit agreement.
These are legally separate:
Service agreement: cancelled with 30 days notice (monthly rolling) or via early
termination charge calculation above (fixed term).
Handset credit agreement: the remaining balance of the handset must be settled in full
on cancellation. Early settlement of a regulated credit agreement incurs no penalty
under the Consumer Credit Act — the customer simply pays the outstanding balance.
The outstanding handset balance is shown in the VoltMobile app under My Agreement.
AGENT NOTE: the total early exit cost for a phone plan is the service early termination
charge PLUS the handset settlement balance. Customers frequently underestimate this.
Always quote both figures clearly and separately so the customer can make an informed decision.
Never quote only the service ETC without mentioning the handset settlement.

PART 7 — BILLING, CHARGES AND DISPUTES

BILLING CYCLE AND PAYMENT

VoltMobile bills monthly. Your billing date is the anniversary of your contract start date.
Bills are issued by email and are visible in the VoltMobile app and at voltex.co.uk/my-account.
Payment is collected by Direct Debit or continuous card payment 7 days after the bill date.
This 7-day gap gives customers time to review their bill and raise any disputes before payment.

UNDERSTANDING YOUR BILL

Your monthly bill contains:
  - Base plan charge: your standard monthly plan cost
  - Any add-ons activated during the month (data boosts, roaming passes, calling add-ons)
  - Out-of-allowance charges (roaming charges not covered by a pass; premium rate calls;
    international calls from the UK)
  - Any one-off charges (late payment fee, SIM replacement fee)
  - Credit adjustments (goodwill credits, compensation credits)

LATE PAYMENT

If payment fails on the collection date:
Day 1: payment failure. Automated retry in 3 days.
Day 4: second attempt. If failed, SMS and email notification sent.
Day 10: third and final attempt. If failed, a £5 late payment fee is applied
and the account enters a restricted state.
Restricted state: outgoing calls and data are suspended. Incoming calls and texts
remain active. The customer can reinstate by paying the overdue balance plus the £5 fee
via the VoltMobile app, at a Voltex store, or by calling 0333 400 9000.
Day 30: if balance remains unpaid, the account is suspended entirely (no incoming or outgoing).
Day 60: account terminated. Debt passed to collections. Number permanently lost.
AGENT NOTE: if a customer is in a restricted or suspended state and contacts us, do not
refuse to help — take the payment on the call and reinstate the service immediately.
If a customer explains a genuine hardship, a 7-day payment extension can be granted
once per 12-month period by Team Leader authorisation. Document the reason in CRM.

PREMIUM RATE CALLS AND THIRD-PARTY CHARGES

Premium rate numbers (09xx, 087x in some cases) are charged at rates set by the
number owner, not by VoltMobile. These are not included in any plan allowance.
Common sources: competition lines, some customer service numbers, adult content services.
Third-party charges: some services charge to a customer's phone bill with their consent
(mobile payment for parking, charity donations, content subscriptions via carrier billing).
These charges appear on the VoltMobile bill as "Third-Party Service Charge."
If a customer does not recognise a third-party charge:
  Step 1: check whether the customer or another user of the phone may have consented.
  Step 2: identify the service provider — the bill shows the name of the third-party.
  Step 3: advise the customer to contact the third-party directly to cancel and request refund.
  Step 4: if the customer believes the charge is fraudulent (no consent given), VoltMobile
    can apply a third-party charge bar to the account (prevents future third-party billing)
    and raise a dispute with the third-party on the customer's behalf.
  Third-party charge bar: free. Applied immediately by the agent on request.
  Refund timeline: third-party disputes take 14 to 28 business days to resolve.
  Goodwill credit: Team Leader can approve a goodwill credit up to £20 while the dispute
    is pending if the charge is significant and the customer is in genuine hardship.

BILLING DISPUTES — PROCESS

If a customer believes a charge on their bill is incorrect:
  Step 1: agent reviews the bill with the customer and identifies the disputed charge.
  Step 2: agent checks the account for corresponding usage records (call logs, data usage,
    roaming sessions) to determine whether the charge is supported by usage data.
  Step 3a: if usage data does not support the charge — this is a billing error.
    Credit the disputed amount immediately. Log in CRM as billing error.
    Escalate to billing team for root cause investigation.
  Step 3b: if usage data supports the charge — explain to the customer what the charge
    relates to. If the customer maintains the charge is incorrect or disputes the usage:
    log as a formal billing dispute. The billing team will investigate within 5 business days.
  Step 3c: if the charge relates to roaming and the customer was unaware of roaming costs:
    check whether the customer was on a plan where roaming should have been included.
    If not, check whether the customer received the standard pre-travel roaming advisory
    SMS (VoltMobile sends this automatically when a SIM connects to a foreign network).
    If the advisory was sent and the customer still incurred charges, a partial goodwill
    credit may be appropriate at Team Leader discretion — not automatic.
    If the advisory was NOT sent due to a system error, full credit of roaming charges
    is appropriate and should be authorised by Team Leader without dispute.
IMPORTANT: never tell a customer a charge is correct without first pulling up their
actual usage records in CRM. Customers who are told their bill is correct without
investigation and later discover an error will escalate to a formal complaint.

PART 8 — DEVICE PROTECTION: VOLTMOBILE VS VOLTCARE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Customers with a phone on VoltMobile may also have VoltCare on their handset.
These are separate products covering different risks. Agents must understand the
distinction clearly to direct claims correctly.

VOLTCARE ON A VOLTMOBILE HANDSET

VoltCare Plus or Complete on a phone covers:
  - Mechanical and electrical breakdown
  - Accidental damage (screen cracks, liquid damage, drops)
VoltCare does NOT cover:
  - Theft or loss
  - Damage while abroad beyond what accidental damage covers

VOLTMOBILE DEVICE INSURANCE (sold separately via Duologi Insurance)

Available as an add-on at checkout when purchasing a phone plan. From £7.99/month.
Covers: theft, loss, accidental damage, breakdown (including when abroad).
This is a separate insurance product underwritten by Duologi Insurance Ltd.
Claims are handled by Duologi Insurance, not by Voltex or VoltMobile.
VoltMobile device insurance claim line: 0333 400 9001.

WHICH PRODUCT TO USE FOR WHICH CLAIM

Breakdown fault (phone stopped working, battery failure, hardware fault):
  → VoltCare claim if the customer has VoltCare. Handled by Voltex repair team.
  → VoltMobile device insurance if the customer does not have VoltCare. Handled by Duologi.
Accidental damage (cracked screen, liquid damage):
  → VoltCare Plus or Complete if the customer has it. Handled by Voltex.
  → VoltMobile device insurance if the customer has it and no VoltCare. Handled by Duologi.
  → If the customer has both: VoltCare is typically faster and has no insurance excess
    (only the VoltCare excess applies). Advise the customer to use VoltCare first.
Theft or loss:
  → VoltMobile device insurance ONLY. VoltCare does not cover theft or loss.
  → If the customer does not have VoltMobile device insurance, this is a matter for
    their home contents insurance.
  → Agents must also advise the customer to: (1) report the theft to the police and
    obtain a crime reference number — required by Duologi Insurance for theft claims;
    (2) contact VoltMobile to bar the SIM immediately to prevent fraudulent usage
    charges. SIM barring is immediate and free — done by agent on the call.

SIM BARRING FOR LOST OR STOLEN PHONES

If a customer reports their phone as lost or stolen, bar the SIM immediately:
This prevents fraudulent calls, texts, and data usage being charged to the account.
Usage charges incurred before the bar is applied are the customer's responsibility.
Usage charges after the bar is confirmed as applied are VoltMobile's responsibility
and will be credited automatically.
The customer's number is preserved for 90 days while the SIM is barred.
If the phone is recovered within 90 days: the SIM can be reinstated by calling
0333 400 9000. The customer's number and account are fully restored.
After 90 days with no reinstatement request: the number is released back to the
number pool and cannot be recovered. A new SIM is issued if the customer wishes to continue.

PART 9 — COMMON VOLTMOBILE QUESTIONS

Q: I have no signal at home — what can I do?
A: Check voltex.co.uk/voltmobile-coverage for your postcode. If coverage is shown as
good in your area, the issue may be building-related — thick walls and basement
locations significantly reduce signal indoors. EE's network offers Wi-Fi Calling on
VoltMobile — enabling this in your phone settings allows calls and texts over your
home broadband connection when mobile signal is weak. Most modern Android and iPhone
devices support Wi-Fi Calling. To enable: on iPhone, go to Settings > Phone > Wi-Fi Calling.
On Android: Settings > Connections > Wi-Fi Calling (location varies by manufacturer).
If your postcode shows limited or no coverage, this is a genuine gap in the EE network.
VoltMobile cannot guarantee coverage at a specific location — if the coverage gap is
persistent and the customer purchased the plan believing coverage was adequate, they
may be entitled to exit their contract without an early termination charge. Escalate
to Team Leader for assessment — this is a contract viability decision.

Q: My phone says "No Service" or "Emergency Calls Only" — what does that mean?
A: "Emergency Calls Only" means the phone can detect a signal but your SIM is not
being recognised by the network. This is different from having no signal.
Common causes:
  - SIM not seated correctly in the SIM tray — ask the customer to remove and reinsert
  - SIM damaged or faulty — a free SIM replacement can be sent (2 to 3 business days)
    or collected from a Voltex store
  - Account suspended due to non-payment — check account status in CRM
  - Very rare: network authentication issue — can be resolved by the technical team;
    a network refresh can be triggered remotely in some cases
If the customer has recently moved to a new phone: confirm the SIM has been physically
transferred and that the new phone is not carrier-locked to a different network.

Q: Why is my 5G not working even though I have a 5G phone?
A: Several conditions must all be true for 5G to work:
  1. The customer must be on a VoltMobile plan that includes 5G (30GB and above, or
     lower plans with the 5G add-on)
  2. The phone must be a 5G-capable device
  3. The customer must be in an area with VoltMobile 5G coverage (check coverage map)
  4. 5G must be enabled in the phone settings (Settings > Mobile Network > Network Mode
     should show 5G/4G/3G/2G Auto — not 4G only)
If all four are true and 5G still does not show: this is a network or device issue.
Ask the customer to restart the phone. If the issue persists, escalate to technical support.

Q: I have been charged for roaming but I never left the UK
A: This is extremely rare but can occur in areas near international borders where
a device inadvertently connects to a foreign network (e.g. near the Northern Ireland
border, on a ferry in the English Channel before leaving UK waters, in a coastal area
with signal from a nearby French or Irish mast).
If the customer is certain they did not leave the UK: check the roaming session records
in CRM. If the roaming session shows a timestamp and location consistent with border
proximity, this is a genuine if unintentional roaming event. Team Leader can approve
a goodwill credit for border-proximity roaming charges — this is assessed case by case.

Q: What happens to my number if I cancel VoltMobile?
A: Your number remains active on VoltMobile until the cancellation takes effect.
If you want to take your number to a new network, request a PAC code before your
VoltMobile service ends — PAC codes can be used to port the number to the new network
after cancellation takes effect. The number remains portable for 30 days from the
PAC issue date, even after the VoltMobile account has closed.
If you do not port the number and simply cancel: the number is deactivated and returns
to the number pool. It cannot be recovered after deactivation.
